\section{Related Work}

Alignment techniques, particularly supervised fine-tuning~\citep{askell2021general,ouyang2022training}, have witnessed remarkable advancements in their ability to effectively leverage the knowledge within large models and enhance human interaction in natural language. The latest studies have predominantly concentrated on investigating alignment techniques in the creation of supervised instruction data~\citep{honovich2022unnatural} and different choices of large language models (e.g.,~\citep{touvron2023llama}). %\omer{I don't understand the second half of this sentence}\pfliu{rewording a little bit but feel free to make further edit}
The commonality of these works lies in either using OpenAI products (e.g., ChatGPT)\footnote{\url{https://chat.openai.com/}} to \emph{distill} instructions~\citep{alpaca,vicuna2023} or using a large number of instructions for supervised fine-tuning~\citep{beeching2023stackllama,sun2023principledriven,openassistant}.
For example, Alpaca~\citep{alpaca} underwent fine-tuning using 52,000 self-instructed instructions~\citep{wang2022selfinstruct}.

OpenAssistant~\citep{openassistant} was trained using roughly 160,000 messages containing assistant-style conversations, which were both generated and annotated by humans.
